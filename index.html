Frequently asked

Good to know.

The questions we hear most often - and the honest answers.

Does my crew need their own account?
No. You create projects and share a read-only link per stage or view. Anyone with the link sees what's happening in realtime - no login, no app store, no hassle.
Is there a free trial?
No separate trial. Pick the plan that matches the number of stages you actually run - Freelancer (1 stage) from €12,95, Team (3 stages), or Festival (unlimited). Running a one-off event? Buy an event pass instead and skip the subscription entirely. Cancel any subscription in one click via Stripe.
Does it work on a backstage crew iPad?
Yes. Stagetimer runs in any modern browser - iOS Safari, Android Chrome, desktop, anything. The backstage and stage views are specifically designed to live permanently on a tablet or monitor.
Can I manage multiple festivals at once?
Yes. Unlimited projects on every plan. A project can contain multiple stages and multiple days, so anything from a one-day conference to a three-day festival with 10+ stages runs on the same subscription.
What happens to my data if I cancel?
You keep access until the end of your last paid period. After that, we set the account to read-only for 30 days so you can export your data. We then delete the project data from our database.
Does it work without internet?
Stagetimer is realtime and syncs over the internet. The last state stays cached in the browser to bridge brief hiccups, but for live work we recommend a stable connection (4G or WiFi) on every device.
Can the operator send messages to the speaker mid-show?
Yes. The floor cue panel sends colour-coded messages (READY, 5 MIN, WRAP UP, STAND BY, GO TO BREAK or any custom text) to the Stage View. The message flashes as a full-bleed banner and auto-clears after 8 seconds. Per-stage scoped, so a cue for one stage never lights up another.
Does it support broadcast rundowns (LIVE / VTR / OOV / GFX / INT / BREAK)?
Yes. Each item in the schedule can carry a broadcast type tag. The badges are colour-coded on every list - Backstage, FOH, schedule, timeline - so a gallery director can scan the rundown by type rather than reading guest names.
Can I extend the live show on the fly?
Tap +5, +1, -1 or -5 min while the show is running. The next slot absorbs the change - its start moves with the live end while its end stays fixed - so only that one slot gets shorter or longer. The rest of the schedule isn't touched.
What if I need to black out the screens during a break?
One Blackout button in the operator dashboard. Every public viewer screen (Stage View, Backstage, FOH) goes black until you flip it off. The operator dashboard stays live so you can keep driving the schedule behind the curtain.
